Operational Performance Measurement Framework

A comprehensive methodology for measuring manufacturing performance through time-based decomposition, hierarchical efficiency metrics, and constraint-state-dependent financial valuation.

This page was created by maji, majaco's AI operational excellence tool. maji is in active development, so occasional inaccuracies may appear as the system continues to learn.

Why Time-Based Decomposition?

Traditional OEE (Overall Equipment Effectiveness) uses a multiplicative framework: Availability × Performance × Quality. This produces a single number that obscures the relative magnitude of different loss types.

Time-based additive decomposition expresses every loss as a duration of time — making losses directly comparable, additive, and easily converted to financial impact.

The result is a framework where managers can say: "We lost 47 minutes to slow running, 23 minutes to unplanned downtime, and 12 minutes to quality waste" — and immediately prioritise the largest opportunity.

The Time Hierarchy

Time CategoryDefinitionAccountability
Calendar Time 24 hours × 7 days × 52 weeks = 8,760 hours/year Fixed
Non-working Time Weekends, bank holidays, planned shutdown Business/Commercial
Shift Time (Available Time) Calendar Time − Non-working Time Operational Management
Schedule Loss Shifts not scheduled — planned but not run Commercial
Planned Production Time Shift Time − Schedule Loss Operational Management
Planned Downtime Planned maintenance, changeovers, breaks Engineering/Planning
Operating Time Planned Production Time − Planned Downtime Shop Floor
Unplanned Downtime (UPDT) Breakdowns, stoppages, waiting for materials Shop Floor/Engineering
Up Time Operating Time − Unplanned Downtime Shop Floor
Lost Time Slow Running (LTSR) Time equivalent of speed loss below target rate Shop Floor
Lost Time Making Waste (LTMW) Time equivalent of quality losses Shop Floor/Quality
Potential Time Up Time − LTSR − LTMW Outcome

Key Mathematical Relationships

Available Time = Calendar Time − Non-working Time
Planned Production Time = Available Time − Schedule Loss
Operating Time = Planned Production Time − Planned Downtime
Up Time = Operating Time − Unplanned Downtime
Potential Time = Up Time − LTSR − LTMW
Total Losses = UPDT + LTSR + LTMW + Planned DT + Schedule Loss

Efficiency Metrics Hierarchy

The correct efficiency metric depends on the constraint state of the line:

MetricFormulaWhen to use
Operating Efficiency Potential Time / Operating Time Shop floor — losses within the shift
Machine Efficiency (OOE) Potential Time / Shift Time Operational management — all planned and unplanned losses
Labour Efficiency (PPLH) Machine Efficiency × (Potential Crew / Actual Crew) Sales-constrained environments — where labour drives profit
TEEP Potential Time / Calendar Time Production-constrained environments — where every hour of capacity has value

Constraint State Determines Framework Selection

The financial value of a lost hour depends on whether the line is the system constraint and whether demand exceeds capacity.

  • Production-constrained (demand > capacity): Every lost hour of potential time = lost throughput at full margin. Use TEEP. Financial value = Throughput Rate × Lost Hours.
  • Sales-constrained (capacity > demand): Lost hours reduce labour efficiency but do not directly reduce sales. Use Labour Efficiency (PPLH). Financial value = Labour Cost × Lost Hours.
