Operational Performance Measurement Framework

A comprehensive methodology for measuring manufacturing performance through time-based decomposition, hierarchical efficiency metrics, and constraint-state-dependent financial valuation.

This page was created by maji, majaco's AI operational excellence tool. maji is in active development, so occasional inaccuracies may appear as the system continues to learn.

Why Time-Based Decomposition?

Traditional OEE uses a multiplicative framework: Availability × Performance × Quality. This produces a single number that obscures the relative magnitude of different loss types.

Time-based additive decomposition expresses every loss as a duration of time — making losses directly comparable, additive, and easily converted to financial impact.

Key Mathematical Relationships

Available Time: Calendar Time − Non-working Time
Planned Production Time: Shift Time − Schedule Loss
Operating Time (Planned): Planned Production Time − Planned Downtime
Operating Time (Actual): Up Time + Unplanned Downtime
Up Time: Potential Time + Lost Time Slow Running + Lost Time Making Waste
Potential Time: Good Output / Bottleneck Speed

1. Operating Efficiency (Shop Floor)

Operating Efficiency = Potential Time / Operating Time

Numerator
Denominator

Critical distinction: Measures shop floor execution within the operational window provided by planning. Shop floor teams control UPDT, LTSR, and LTMW but cannot influence Schedule Loss or Planned Downtime standards. World-class target: ≥85%.

2. Machine Efficiency / OOE (Operational Management)

Machine Efficiency = Potential Time / Shift Time

Numerator
Denominator

Critical distinction: Extends accountability beyond shop floor to include Schedule Loss design and Planned Downtime standards. Terminology varies by constraint state: "Machine Efficiency" (sales-constrained cost focus) vs "OOE" (production-constrained throughput focus). Formula identical.

3. TEEP (Strategic Leadership)

TEEP = Potential Time / Calendar Time

Numerator
Denominator

Use when production-constrained: Market demand exceeds production capacity. Every unit of additional capacity translates to additional sales at full margin.

Note: Machine Efficiency and OOE are mathematically identical; terminology reflects strategic context. Labour Efficiency formula: Machine Efficiency × (Potential Crew / Actual Crew). TEEP formula abbreviations: PT=Potential Time, OT=Operating Time (Actual), ST=Shift Time, CT=Calendar Time, PDT=Planned Downtime.

Constraint State Determines Framework Selection

The financial value of a lost hour depends on whether the line is the system constraint and whether demand exceeds capacity.

  • Production-constrained (demand > capacity): Every lost hour of potential time = lost throughput at full margin. Use TEEP.
  • Sales-constrained (capacity > demand): Lost hours reduce labour efficiency but do not directly reduce sales. Use Labour Efficiency (PPLH).
