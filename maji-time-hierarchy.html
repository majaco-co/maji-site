Operational Performance Measurement Framework

A comprehensive methodology for measuring manufacturing performance through time-based decomposition, hierarchical efficiency metrics, and constraint-state-dependent financial valuation.

This page was created by maji, majaco's AI operational excellence tool. maji is in active development, so occasional inaccuracies may appear as the system continues to learn.

Why Time-Based Decomposition?

Traditional OEE uses a multiplicative framework: Availability × Performance × Quality. This produces a single number that obscures the relative magnitude of different loss types.

Time-based additive decomposition expresses every loss as a duration of time — making losses directly comparable, additive, and easily converted to financial impact.

Key Mathematical Relationships

Available Time = Calendar Time − Non-working Time
Planned Production Time = Available Time − Schedule Loss
Operating Time = Planned Production Time − Planned Downtime
Up Time = Operating Time − Unplanned Downtime
Potential Time = Up Time − LTSR − LTMW
Total Losses = UPDT + LTSR + LTMW + Planned DT + Schedule Loss

Efficiency Metrics Hierarchy

The correct efficiency metric depends on the constraint state of the line:

MetricFormulaWhen to use
Operating Efficiency Potential Time / Operating Time Shop floor — losses within the shift
Machine Efficiency (OOE) Potential Time / Shift Time Operational management — all planned and unplanned losses
Labour Efficiency (PPLH) Machine Efficiency × (Potential Crew / Actual Crew) Sales-constrained environments
TEEP Potential Time / Calendar Time Production-constrained environments

Constraint State Determines Framework Selection

The financial value of a lost hour depends on whether the line is the system constraint and whether demand exceeds capacity.

  • Production-constrained (demand > capacity): Every lost hour of potential time = lost throughput at full margin. Use TEEP.
  • Sales-constrained (capacity > demand): Lost hours reduce labour efficiency but do not directly reduce sales. Use Labour Efficiency (PPLH).
