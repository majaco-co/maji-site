Redzone x majaco

Partnership Workshop Output

Accelerating win-win-win outcomes for Redzone, majaco and their shared clients. This document captures the strategic rationale, commercial model, target pipeline and next steps agreed during the March 2026 partnership workshop with Ryan Marshall (Senior Director, Global Accounts, QAD Redzone).

The Partnership

majaco and QAD Redzone formalised a Professional Services Agreement in December 2025. Under this agreement, majaco delivers productivity module coaching for Redzone customers — typically four-week deployments covering system setup, huddle structures, change management and operator training.

This document sets out how the partnership goes further: beyond coaching deployments into joint commercial propositions that combine Redzone's connected workforce platform with majaco's operational improvement methodology and results guarantee.

The two businesses are complementary, not competitive. Redzone provides the connected workforce platform — real-time downtime tracking, OEE measurement, and in-run PPLH measurement where headcount is tracked in Redzone, huddle facilitation, workforce engagement. majaco provides the analytical methodology — Lost Time decomposition, Split Solving, labour planning science — and the commercial guarantee that ensures identified savings are actually realised. Neither can fully deliver the other's value.

The core insight: Redzone enables the data capture and frontline engagement that underpins sustainable improvement. majaco adds two things Redzone cannot deliver alone. First, majaco solves the technical problems behind the biggest opportunities the data reveals — lost time from slow running, downtime (often short stops and holds) — and critically helps clients see the true potential versus perfect, because without accurate speed targets clients will continue to hide slow running behind soft Redzone settings. Second, majaco ensures that the productivity gains identified through Redzone data are converted into real cost reductions through planning rate changes, shift restructuring and headcount adjustments. Without these steps, OEE improvements remain theoretical.

Win for Clients

A guaranteed return on their Connected Workforce System spend. A single programme combining Redzone's real-time visibility with majaco's analytical rigour — de-risked by a joint fee guarantee. Redzone properly configured and embedded from day one.

Win for Redzone

Accelerated access to £500M–£1Bn FMCG clients through majaco's trusted relationships. Help closing accounts where clients are wary of not getting ROI on a large Redzone spend, where adoption has been patchy, or where coaching cost is an objection.

Win for majaco

Strengthened pipeline through Redzone's brand advocacy and network introductions. A technology-integrated offering that embeds sustainability. Ongoing licence revenue share and coaching revenue as majaco scales to 30 people.

The Joint Proposition

The headline for the client is simple: a guaranteed return on their Connected Workforce System spend. Clients considering Redzone often face internal resistance around the licence cost and uncertainty of ROI. The joint proposition eliminates that risk — the combined majaco + Redzone fee is covered by the guarantee, so the client only pays in full if the results materialise.

majaco's existing model guarantees a minimum 1:1 return on fees before full payment is triggered, with a 2–3x ROI target. The joint proposition extends this to cover Redzone's licensing costs, creating a single risk-sharing package for the client.

Worked Example

Scenario: majaco identifies £2M labour productivity savings.

Without partnership: majaco would charge £1M and guarantee a minimum 1:1 ROI. Redzone would separately charge £150k licensing, £50k coaching and £50k hardware.

With partnership: majaco charges £1.035M (delivery fees plus £35k of coaching, which majaco provides directly using boot-camp-trained consultants). Redzone charges £150k licensing + £50k hardware. The client saves £15k on coaching and gets a single coordinated programme.

Joint fee guarantee = £1.185M (majaco's £1.035M + Redzone's £150k licensing). Redzone receives the first £150k of guaranteed savings — so if the joint programme delivers only £150k annualised, Redzone gets paid in full and majaco receives nothing. The £50k hardware is a one-off capital cost for the client, which Redzone sorts with them independently and sits outside the guarantee.

Why It Delivers

majaco guarantees labour productivity improvements of 10–50%, translating to approximately 10–33% cost reduction per unit. Results are measured using a variance-to-standard methodology that normalises for product mix — improvements are signed off by the client's site finance team.

Where the Gains Come From

Typically, total productivity improvement is split roughly 50/50 between two pillars:

Pillar What It Covers Redzone Role
OEE / Equipment Efficiency Lost Time reduction (LTSR, LTMW, UPDT), speed optimisation, downtime Pareto resolution via Split Solving Primary data source for downtime tracking, run segments, stop reasons. Enables Pareto analysis and trend monitoring.
Planning & Labour Control Crewing to the correct planning standard, shift utilisation, indirect workforce planning, planning rate increases to realise OEE benefits PPLH data from run segments supports planning rate calibration. Huddle structures embed daily labour accountability.

The critical gap Redzone cannot close alone: To realise the benefits of OEE improvement, planned rates must be increased, shifts must be planned with more work, and changes to headcount or shift patterns may be necessary. Redzone's primary incentive is platform adoption and licence expansion — not ensuring operational benefits are converted into financial results. For majaco, this conversion is the product: we only get paid if we demonstrate results.

Implementation Quality

majaco doesn't just recommend Redzone — we ensure it is properly installed and configured so the client gets the most out of it. majaco led the Redzone implementation at Vibrant Foods, which was described by Redzone coach Neil West as "a benchmark for us to compare future rollouts to", and has since led follow-on implementations at two further sites.

Through our implementation experience, we consistently see the same pitfalls derailing CWS rollouts. majaco's role is to prevent these from the outset:

Common Pitfall What Goes Wrong How majaco Prevents It
Poor reference data setup Products lack clear groupings, planning rate alignment and true bottleneck speeds. Downtime codes are inconsistent across sites, not mutually exclusive, or missing "planned" duration agreements. majaco builds the product hierarchy and downtime tree as part of the assessment, aligned to the Lost Time framework (LTSR, LTMW, UPDT). Codes are designed to be mutually exclusive, completely exhaustive, and consistent across lines and sites from day one.
Poor automation setup Wrong locations automated, sub-optimal signals chosen, or too many locations left manual — leading to inaccurate data that operators stop trusting. majaco specifies automation requirements during the assessment, ensuring signals are validated against observed performance before go-live.
Not engaging the whole team Certain departments aren't included or enabled, those individuals disengage. Digital transformation requires whole-team buy-in from well before go-live. majaco's change management programme identifies all affected team members and engages them from day one. Huddle structures, shift reviews and town halls are designed to include every level of the organisation.
Insufficient buy-in to meetings and reporting Legacy reporting methods persist alongside the new system, creating duplication and undermining adoption. The system is "installed but not embedded". majaco builds a bespoke analytics layer on top of Redzone data (typically Power BI), tailored to each level of the organisation. This replaces legacy reporting entirely and ensures long-term sustainability.

The client message: With majaco, you don't just get a Redzone licence and a coaching manual. You get a team that has delivered the best Redzone rollout in the UK, who will configure the system properly, build the reporting you actually need, embed the behaviours that make it stick, and guarantee the financial results.

Commercial Structure

Existing Agreements

A Referral Partner Agreement is in place (signed 26 June 2025, term to 25 July 2026) under which QAD pays majaco 15% of net subscription fees in year 1 and 10% in year 2 for any new customer majaco refers into Redzone. The agreement covers Europe, applies to new customers only, and requires QAD to accept the lead and close within 180 days. Donnelly Fresh Foods is the first client this applies to.

Proposed Terms

For the joint guarantee model, majaco would seek to formalise an ongoing share of Redzone licensing fees (indicatively 15%) for any client where the guarantee is deployed. This goes beyond simple referral — it reflects the role majaco plays in both selling the platform and ensuring it delivers sustained value. For Redzone, it aligns incentives: majaco is motivated to ensure the platform sticks, not just to complete and walk away.

  • Key pain points: Sustainability of improvements when key people leave; data consolidation ("more data than sense"); raw material variability in fresh produce; high cost of Redzone coaching.
  • majaco differentiator: Single contract coordinating both majaco and Redzone. Boot-camp-trained consultants provide coaching without duplicating Redzone's charges. Guarantee-based pricing removes the ROI objection.
  • History: Previously worked with Newton Europe (~20-person team) with positive results, but sustainability was the core challenge. Several ex-Newton staff still at IPL.
