Redzone x majaco

Partnership Workshop Output

Accelerating win-win-win outcomes for Redzone, majaco and their shared clients. This document captures the strategic rationale, commercial model, target pipeline and next steps agreed during the March 2026 partnership workshop with Ryan Marshall (Senior Director, Global Accounts, QAD Redzone).

Partnership Context

majaco and QAD Redzone formalised a Professional Services Agreement in December 2025. Under this agreement, majaco delivers productivity module coaching for Redzone customers — typically four-week deployments covering system setup, huddle structures, change management and operator training. majaco consultants complete a two-week QAD boot camp and shadow existing Redzone coaches before deploying independently.

This workshop explored how the partnership can go further: beyond coaching deployments into joint commercial propositions that combine Redzone's connected workforce platform with majaco's operational improvement methodology and results guarantee.

Why This Partnership Works

Redzone want to accelerate their penetration into the £500M–£1Bn turnover segment — precisely majaco's target client profile. majaco's challenge is capacity: the team plans to scale from its current size to approximately 30 people over the next three to four years, so this is a long-term partnership to nurture, not a short-term volume play.

The two businesses are complementary, not competitive. Redzone provides the connected workforce platform (real-time downtime tracking, OEE measurement, and in-shift PPLH measurement where headcount is tracked in Redzone, huddle facilitation, workforce engagement). majaco provides the analytical methodology (Lost Time decomposition, Split Solving, labour planning science) and the commercial guarantee that ensures identified savings are actually realised. Neither can fully deliver the other's value.

Win for Clients

A guaranteed return on their Connected Workforce System spend. A single programme combining Redzone's real-time visibility with majaco's analytical rigour in planning, labour control and OEE improvement — de-risked by a joint fee guarantee. Redzone properly configured and embedded from day one.

Win for Redzone

Accelerated access to £500M–£1Bn FMCG clients through majaco's trusted relationships and senior-level network. Deeper adoption and proven ROI through majaco's delivery guarantee. Help closing accounts where clients are wary of not getting ROI on a large Redzone spend, where adoption has been patchy, or where coaching cost is an objection.

Win for majaco

Strengthened pipeline through Redzone's brand advocacy and network introductions. A technology-integrated offering that embeds sustainability. Ongoing licence revenue share. Coaching revenue from boot-camp-trained consultants. Long-term funnel development as majaco scales to 30 people.

The core insight: Redzone enables the data capture and frontline engagement that underpins sustainable improvement. majaco ensures that the productivity gains identified through Redzone data are converted into real cost reductions through planning rate changes, shift restructuring and headcount adjustments. Without this second step, OEE improvements remain theoretical.

How majaco Delivers Results

The Productivity Equation

majaco guarantees labour productivity improvements of 10–50%, translating to approximately 10–33% cost reduction per unit. Results are measured using a variance-to-standard methodology that normalises for product mix, ensuring improvements reflect genuine efficiency gains rather than favourable mix shifts.

Worked example (baseline): A site produces 1,000 units in 400 labour hours (300 direct + 100 indirect), against a direct standard of 250 hours. Baseline productivity = 1,000 / 400 = 2.5 units per labour hour.

After improvement: 1,200 units in 385 hours (280 direct + 105 indirect), against 300 direct standard hours (same mix). Improved productivity = 1,200 / 385 = 3.12 units per labour hour. That is a 24% productivity increase, equating to a 20% reduction in spend per unit.

These improvements are signed off by the client's site finance team. The measurement methodology strips out the impact of cost-per-hour changes and accounts for mix variation through accurate standards.

Where the Gains Come From

Typically, total productivity improvement is split roughly 50/50 between two pillars:

Pillar What It Covers Redzone Role
OEE / Equipment Efficiency Lost Time reduction (LTSR, LTMW, UPDT), speed optimisation, downtime Pareto resolution via Split Solving Primary data source for downtime tracking, run segments, stop reasons. Enables Pareto analysis and trend monitoring.

The critical gap Redzone cannot close alone: To realise the benefits of OEE improvement, planned rates must be increased, shifts must be planned with more work, and changes to headcount or shift patterns may be necessary. Redzone's primary incentive is platform adoption and licence expansion — not ensuring operational benefits are converted into financial results. For majaco, this conversion is the product: we only get paid if we demonstrate results.

Joint Commercial Model

The Client Proposition: Guaranteed CWS Return

The headline for the client is simple: a guaranteed return on their Connected Workforce System spend. Clients considering Redzone often face internal resistance around the licence cost and uncertainty of ROI. The joint proposition eliminates that risk entirely — the combined majaco + Redzone fee is covered by the guarantee, so the client only pays in full if the results materialise.

majaco's existing model guarantees a minimum 1:1 return on fees before full payment is triggered, with a 2–3x ROI target. The joint proposition extends this to cover Redzone's costs, creating a single risk-sharing package for the client.

Example scenario: majaco identifies £2M labour productivity savings.

Without partnership: majaco would charge £1M and guarantee a minimum 1:1 ROI. Redzone would separately charge £150k licensing, £50k coaching and £50k hardware.

With partnership: majaco charges £1.035M (delivery fees plus £35k of coaching, which majaco provides directly using boot-camp-trained consultants). Redzone charges £150k licensing + £50k hardware. The client saves £15k on coaching and gets a single coordinated programme.

Joint fee guarantee = £1.185M (majaco's £1.035M + Redzone's £150k licensing). Redzone receives the first £150k of guaranteed savings — so if the joint programme delivers only £150k annualised, Redzone gets paid in full and majaco receives nothing. The £50k hardware is a one-off capital cost for the client, which Redzone sorts with them independently and sits outside the guarantee.

How the guarantee waterfall works (first £2M of savings delivered):
RZ £150k
majaco £1,035k
Client net benefit £815k

Redzone receives the first £150k tranche — lowest risk, but still contingent on the joint solution working. At £1.185M delivered, both parties have earned their full fee. Hardware (£50k capex) sits outside the guarantee. If savings fall short, majaco continues working at own cost until the guaranteed run-rate is demonstrated.

This structure means Redzone takes the least risk (first £100k tranche) but is still dependent on majaco's delivery and the joint solution working. It is not a 90-day money-back trial — if we recommend Redzone, we believe it is the right solution and commit to achieving the target productivity gain outlined in the assessment.

What majaco Delivers for Redzone

majaco will provide coaching resource directly (using boot-camp-trained consultants), passing on a portion of the coaching cost saving to the client. majaco will also ensure that Redzone is configured correctly for the site — so that the platform delivers value from day one rather than requiring a separate, costly coaching engagement.

Critically, for clients where Redzone is struggling to close or where adoption has been patchy (IPL is a live example), majaco's guarantee-backed approach removes the objection. This helps Redzone sell into accounts they would otherwise lose, which is a significant short-term win for their licence revenue.

Ensuring a World-Class Implementation

A key part of the client sell is that majaco doesn't just recommend Redzone — we ensure it is properly installed and configured so the client gets the most out of it. majaco led the Redzone implementation at Vibrant Foods, which was described by Redzone coach Neil West as "a benchmark for us to compare future rollouts to", and has since led follow-on implementations at two further sites. This experience, combined with prior development of an award-winning IoT factory line monitoring system, means majaco understands what good looks like — and what goes wrong.

Through our implementation experience, we consistently see the same pitfalls derailing CWS rollouts. majaco's role is to prevent these from the outset:

Common Pitfall What Goes Wrong How majaco Prevents It
Poor reference data setup Products lack clear groupings, planning rate alignment and true bottleneck speeds. Downtime codes are inconsistent across sites, not mutually exclusive, or missing "planned" duration agreements. This only worsens over time and is difficult to fix retrospectively. majaco builds the product hierarchy and downtime tree as part of the assessment, aligned to the Lost Time framework (LTSR, LTMW, UPDT). Codes are designed to be mutually exclusive, completely exhaustive, and consistent across lines and sites from day one.
Poor automation setup Wrong locations automated, sub-optimal signals chosen, or too many locations left manual — leading to inaccurate data that operators stop trusting. majaco specifies automation requirements during the assessment, ensuring signals are validated against observed performance before go-live.
Not engaging the whole team If the system includes meetings, actions and messaging functionality but certain departments aren't included or enabled, those individuals disengage. Digital transformation requires whole-team buy-in from well before go-live. majaco's change management programme identifies all affected team members and engages them from day one. Huddle structures, shift reviews and town halls are designed to include every level of the organisation.
Insufficient buy-in to meetings and reporting Legacy reporting methods persist alongside the new system, creating duplication and undermining adoption. The system is "installed but not embedded". majaco builds a bespoke analytics layer on top of Redzone data (typically Power BI), tailored to each level of the organisation. This replaces legacy reporting entirely and sets the tone for review meetings and action setting — ensuring long-term sustainability.

The client message: With majaco, you don't just get a Redzone licence and a coaching manual. You get a team that has delivered the best Redzone rollout in the UK, who will configure the system properly, build the reporting you actually need, embed the behaviours that make it stick, and guarantee the financial results. That is what de-risks your CWS investment.

Ongoing Commercial Terms

A Referral Partner Agreement is already in place (signed 26 June 2025, term to 25 July 2026) under which QAD pays majaco 15% of net subscription fees in year 1 and 10% in year 2 for any new customer majaco refers into Redzone. Donnelly Fresh Foods is the first client this applies to. The agreement covers Europe, applies to new customers only, and requires QAD to accept the lead and close within 180 days.

For the broader joint proposition described in this document, majaco would seek to formalise an ongoing share of Redzone licensing fees (indicatively 15%) for any client where the joint guarantee model is deployed. This goes beyond simple referral — it reflects the role majaco plays in both selling the platform and ensuring it delivers sustained value. For majaco, this creates a recurring revenue stream that complements programme fees. For Redzone, it aligns incentives — majaco is motivated to ensure the platform sticks, not just to complete and walk away.

Longer-term strategic value for majaco: Beyond programme fees and licence shares, this partnership develops majaco's sales funnel and de-risks new work generation. Each successful Redzone deployment becomes a reference site, a proof point, and a route to further work (yield, waste, supply chain) within the same client. As majaco scales towards 30 people over the next 3–4 years, the Redzone channel could become a meaningful contributor to pipeline.
