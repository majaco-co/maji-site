PoC ≠ Production

The prototypes above validate that your data can power all five models. Moving to production is where the real work begins: backtesting against held-out data to prove predictions are accurate, not just plausible. Cross-validation across regions and time periods to ensure models generalise. Confidence intervals on every recommendation so your ops team knows how much to trust each score. Edge-case handling for new venues, new dealers, sparse data regions, and seasonal anomalies. Proper data pipeline engineering - automated ingestion, validation rules, reconciliation checks, and audit trails rather than a one-off CSV import. The PoC shows the what; the build phase delivers the how well.

03 - Data Capture Strategy

Better Data In,
Better Decisions Out

The models above were built on your existing data. Here's how small changes to data capture will dramatically improve their accuracy and unlock new capabilities.

1
Quick Wins
Weeks 1–4

Standardise Jotform Submissions

Enforce required fields, add postcode validation, replace free-text dealer names with a dropdown. Prevents initials collisions (e.g. two different dealers sharing the same code) and ensures clean linking between events, dealers, and sales.

Capture Customer Postcodes

Even the first 3–4 characters (outward code) is enough. This unlocks demand heatmapping: see where your sellers are actually coming from, not just where the venue is.

Standardise Item Categories

Add a category dropdown to the SKU jotform: Gold, Silver, Watches, Cameras, Medals, Coins, Jewellery, Other. Currently we infer this from free-text titles - a dropdown gives clean data instantly.

Add Footfall Counter

Simple tally of total walk-ins vs. those who sell something. Gives a conversion rate per venue - a powerful signal for the pattern analysis model.

2
Process Integration
Weeks 5–10

Structured Metal Tracking

Replace manual gold/silver tracking with a structured form linked to event number. Eliminates the reconciliation problem between the events sheet and gold_sales/silver_sales tabs.

Dealer Self-Reporting

End-of-day form: items bought, estimated values, categories. Gives per-dealer item-level data - currently only available at event level.

Sale Outcome Linking

When items sell on eBay, Fellows, or trade, link the sale back to the original SKU. Closes the loop from purchase to resale - the missing piece for true margin analysis.

Venue Feedback Loop

Automated post-event venue rating form. Builds the venue scoring database that drives Models 1 and 5.

3
Platform Integration
Weeks 11–14

Google Sheets Sync

Automated daily pull from your existing sheets into the platform database. No manual exports, no CSVs. Your team keeps working in Sheets as they do now.

Jotform Webhooks

New jotform submissions flow directly into the platform database in real-time. Models update overnight on fresh data.

Dashboard Access

Ops team gets web-based access to all model outputs: event calendar, dealer leaderboard, area explorer, capacity dashboard.

Automated Alerting

Weekly email digest: areas ready for return visits, dealer performance flags, emerging white-space opportunities.
