Volume & Planning
◆

Plan Attainment

Did we make what we planned? SKU-level over/under-production drives the Volume and Mix variances.
Plan Attainment % = Actual Good Output ÷ Planned Output (per SKU)
›Over- and under-production flagged by SKU
›Pareto: which SKUs consistently miss plan?
›Links to service level and schedule smoothing
›Consistent misses suggest planning rate standards need review
Labour
◆

Kg per Labour Hour

Actual kg per total factory hour — all roles planned for given volume: line crew, supervision, QA, goods in, dispatch, warehouse, hygiene. The whole-factory productivity metric.
Kg/LH = Actual kg ÷ Total factory hours (all planned roles)
›Includes every role planned for production volume — direct, indirect, goods in, dispatch, warehouse, hygiene (semi-variable)
›Higher volume: expect better Kg/LH — Machine Efficiency rises and indirect staff are better utilised across more output
›Lower volume: the opportunity is how you plan and flex indirect roles — without active flexing, Kg/LH deteriorates as fixed-ish heads spread over fewer kg
›Actual vs Planned: if actual Kg/LH is poor, is it an execution problem or a planning problem? If planned Kg/LH is already low, there was no chance of a good result — we planned in too much labour
›Weekly trend by line and area — underlying metric that drives the efficiency variance
※ Compared against planned Kg/LH — the standard derived from planning rates and manning allowances for all factory roles at the given volume. As Machine Efficiency improves and updated rates feed back into standards, the planned target rises too. A persistently low planned Kg/LH signals a planning problem, not an operational one.
◆

Labour Efficiency Variance (£)

Financial impact of using more or fewer hours than standard for actual output
(Actual hrs − Std hrs for actual output) × Std rate
›Decomposes into Machine Efficiency (line speed & uptime) and Crewing Efficiency (overmanning & indirect)
›Machine Eff × Crewing Eff = total labour efficiency — if the line runs slowly OR we overman, efficiency variance is adverse
›Independent of rate — even expensive hours are efficient if they produce standard output
›Weekly trend by line and area
◆

Labour Rate Variance (£)

Financial impact of paying more or less per hour than standard — agency, overtime, base rate changes
(Actual rate − Std rate) × Actual hrs
›Independent of productivity — even a perfectly crewed shift can have an adverse rate if agency % is too high
›Driven by: agency premium, overtime premium, base rate vs standard
›Split by cost driver: agency %, overtime %, base rate deviation
›Weekly trend by area

Material Usage Variance — MUV (£)

Kg used vs standard kg for actual output — operational efficiency of material consumption
MUV = (Std qty for actual output − Actual qty used) × Std price per kg
Decomposes into: yield variance (quantity) | mix variance (grade substitution)
›Yield variance: actual material consumed vs recipe standard for good output produced
›Mix variance: using higher/lower grade materials than recipe specifies
›MUV is purely operational — valued at standard prices to isolate usage from price
›Pareto by SKU, line, material type — identifies where material usage leaks
◆

Purchase Price Variance — PPV (£)

What we paid per kg vs standard price — procurement-driven, not operational
PPV = (Actual price per kg − Std price per kg) × Actual qty purchased
›Isolated from usage — shows pure price impact from procurement
›Driven by: supplier negotiations, market price moves, spot vs contract buying
›Split by material type / grade / supplier
›Separate from MUV so operations and procurement are accountable for their own variances

◆

Overhead Absorption Variance (£)

Under/over-absorption of fixed overhead driven by actual vs budgeted machine hours
(Actual machine hrs − Budget machine hrs) × OH rate per hr
›Machine hours are the absorption driver — low utilisation means under-recovery of fixed costs
›Directly linked to Machine Efficiency — if lines run fewer hours than budgeted, overhead is under-absorbed
›Volume-driven: more volume = more hours = better absorption (all else equal)
›OH rate per hour set by Finance — reviewed when capacity changes

£/kg Recovery — Commercial Variance
Target £/kg = Weighted average selling price at standard mix
Actual £/kg = Weighted average accounting for downgrades at lower prices
Recovery Gap = Target £/kg − Actual £/kg
This is a commercial / pricing variance, not an operational yield metric. Misshapes drive the quantity; the £ impact sits in the P&L bridge as Commercial Recovery.
Key Equation
Overall Yield % = Saleable Output (kg) ÷ Raw Material Input (kg)
£ Yield Loss = (1 − Overall Yield %) × Input kg × Raw Material £/kg
